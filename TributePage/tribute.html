Muhammad Ali Jinnah

There is no power on earth that can undo Pakistan

Quaid-e-Azam Muhammad Ali Jinnah, often referred to as the "Father of the Nation" in Pakistan, was a visionary leader and statesman who played a pivotal role in the creation of the Islamic Republic of Pakistan. Born on December 25, 1876, in Karachi, which was then part of British India, Jinnah emerged as the chief architect of Pakistan during a tumultuous period of history. His unwavering commitment to the rights of Muslims and his skillful political negotiations led to the establishment of Pakistan as an independent nation on August 14, 1947. Quaid-e-Azam's legacy is marked by his devotion to the principles of democracy, religious freedom, and social justice, which continue to shape the nation's identity and aspirations to this day.
